Research in Depth

Motivation: symmetry violation & new physics

Electric dipole moments (EDMs) and related moments (e.g., nuclear MQMs) probe CP‑violation far beyond collider energies. Molecules offer large internal effective fields, but typical transitions are hypersensitive to ambient E/B noise, which degrades coherence and introduces systematics.

Key idea: Magic (field‑insensitive) molecular clock transitions

We identify pairs of states whose Stark/Zeeman slopes cancel at tunable external fields (E,B), engineering clock‑like transitions with vanishing first‑order sensitivities while preserving large internal CP‑sensitive matrix elements. We refer to these as CP‑FITs (CP‑sensitive, field‑insensitive transitions).

  • Design: Build an effective Hamiltonian (rotational, spin‑rotation, hyperfine, l‑doubling, Stark, Zeeman), diagonalize vs. E,B; locate points where ∂ω/∂E≈0 and ∂ω/∂B≈0.
  • Implementation: Choose polarization/geometries to minimize vector/tensor shifts; exploit parity doublets and hyperfine mixing for tunability.
  • Outcome: Orders‑of‑magnitude suppression of E/B noise while maintaining strong eEDM response; longer coherence; reduced systematics.

Generalization across platforms

The strategy applies to linear and polyatomic molecules (e.g., YbOH bending modes with l‑doubling), molecular ions, and potentially atomic systems with near‑degenerate structure. It interfaces naturally with Ramsey spectroscopy, beam or trapped experiments, and optical cycling readout.

Selected experimental highlights

  • Demonstrated simultaneous suppression of electric and magnetic sensitivities while retaining eEDM response.
  • Validated with intentional E/B noise injection and Ramsey fringe measurements.
  • Outlined extension to MQM‑sensitive platforms and networked sensors.
